Hello people of the inter-webs!

I am a machine learning researcher at Microsoft Research (MSR) Cambridge. My work explores how machine learning can help people with vision impairment. I am particularly interested in how models can be made more robust to real-world scenarios â€“ for example, when only a small amount of training data is available, or the data are poor quality (e.g. blurry images, poor view points). Solving these challenges will enable us to build robust human-in-the-loop systems, where visually-impaired users can provide a small amount of training data themselves, and the system can then be personalised to their everyday interactions on-the-fly.

Prior to joining MSR, I did my D.Phil in computer vision at the University of Oxford under Prof. Philip Torr and Dr Stephen Hicks, my Masters in Neuroscience also at the University of Oxford, and my Bachelors in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Cape Town. I am passionate about diversity and inclusion in machine learning, and am an organiser of the Deep Learning Indaba.
